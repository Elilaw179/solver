1. Area of a Rectangle

The area of a rectangle is calculated by multiplying its length by its width.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width} \)

Example: If a rectangle has a length of 5 meters and a width of 3 meters, its area is \( 5 \times 3 = 15 \) square meters.

2. Area of a Square

The area of a square is calculated by squaring the length of one of its sides.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \text{Side}^2 \)

Example: If a square has a side length of 4 meters, its area is \( 4^2 = 16 \) square meters.

3. Area of a Triangle

The area of a triangle is calculated by multiplying the base by the height and then dividing by 2.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Base} \times \text{Height} \)

Example: If a triangle has a base of 6 meters and a height of 4 meters, its area is \( \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 4 = 12 \) square meters.

4. Area of a Parallelogram

The area of a parallelogram is calculated by multiplying its base by its height.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \text{Base} \times \text{Height} \)

Example: If a parallelogram has a base of 8 meters and a height of 5 meters, its area is \( 8 \times 5 = 40 \) square meters.

5. Area of a Trapezium

The area of a trapezium is calculated by taking the average of the lengths of the two parallel sides and then multiplying by the height.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (\text{Base}_1 + \text{Base}_2) \times \text{Height} \)

Example: If a trapezium has bases of 7 meters and 5 meters, and a height of 3 meters, its area is \( \frac{1}{2} \times (7 + 5) \times 3 = 18 \) square meters.

6. Area of a Circle

The area of a circle is calculated by multiplying pi by the square of the radius.

Formula: \( \text{Area} = \pi \times \text{Radius}^2 \)

Example: If a circle has a radius of 3 meters, its area is \( \pi \times 3^2 = 28.27 \) square meters (using \( \pi \approx 3.14 \)).

7. Circumference of a Circle

The circumference of a circle is calculated by multiplying 2 times pi by the radius.

Formula: \( \text{Circumference} = 2 \times \pi \times \text{Radius} \)

Example: If a circle has a radius of 4 meters, its circumference is \( 2 \times \pi \times 4 = 25.13 \) meters (using \( \pi \approx 3.14 \)).

8. Perimeter of a Rectangle

The perimeter of a rectangle is calculated by adding together twice the length and twice the width.

Formula: \( \text{Perimeter} = 2 \times (\text{Length} + \text{Width}) \)

Example: If a rectangle has a length of 5 meters and a width of 3 meters, its perimeter is \( 2 \times (5 + 3) = 16 \) meters.

9. Perimeter of a Square

The perimeter of a square is calculated by multiplying the length of one of its sides by 4.

Formula: \( \text{Perimeter} = 4 \times \text{Side} \)

Example: If a square has a side length of 4 meters, its perimeter is \( 4 \times 4 = 16 \) meters.
