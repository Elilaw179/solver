Circle Geometry Formulas

1. Circumference of a Circle

The circumference (C) of a circle is given by:
Formula: \( C = 2\pi r \)
Where:

  • r = radius of the circle
  • π (Pi) ≈ 3.14159

2. Area of a Circle

The area (A) of a circle is given by:
Formula: \( A = \pi r^2 \)
Where:

  • r = radius of the circle

3. Arc Length

The length of an arc (L) of a circle is given by:
Formula: \( L = \theta r \)
Where:

  • θ = central angle in radians
  • r = radius of the circle

4. Sector Area

The area (A) of a sector of a circle is given by:
Formula: \( A = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \theta \)
Where:

  • θ = central angle in radians
  • r = radius of the circle

5. Angle in a Semicircle

An angle in a semicircle is always a right angle (90 degrees).

6. Tangent to a Circle

The tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency.

7. The Angle Subtended by the Same Arc

The angle subtended by an arc at the center of a circle is twice the angle subtended at any point on the circumference.

8. Angle at the Center

The angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc is twice the angle subtended at any other point on the circle by the same arc.

Circle Theorems Part2

1. The Angle at the Center Theorem

The angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc is twice the angle subtended at any point on the circumference by the same arc.
Formula: \( \angle AOB = 2 \times \angle ACB \)

2. The Angle in a Semicircle Theorem

The angle subtended by a diameter at the circumference of a circle is a right angle (90 degrees).
Formula: \( \angle ABC = 90^\circ \)

3. Angles in the Same Segment Theorem

Angles subtended by the same chord at the circumference of a circle are equal.
Formula: \( \angle ABC = \angle ADC \)

4. The Opposite Angles of a Cyclic Quadrilateral Theorem

The sum of the opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral (a quadrilateral inscribed in a circle) is 180 degrees.
Formula: \( \angle ABC + \angle CDA = 180^\circ \)

5. The Tangent-Secant Theorem

The angle between a tangent and a chord through the point of contact is equal to the angle subtended by the chord in the alternate segment.
Formula: \( \angle BAC = \angle BDC \)


6. The Alternate Segment Theorem

The angle between the tangent and the chord is equal to the angle in the alternate segment of the circle.
Formula: \( \angle ACB = \angle BDC \)

7. The Perpendicular from the Center to a Chord Theorem

The perpendicular drawn from the center of a circle to a chord bisects the chord.
Formula: \( OM \perp AB \text{ and } AM = MB \)

8. Equal Chords and Their Distances from the Center

Equal chords of a circle are equidistant from the center.
Formula: If \( AB = CD \), then \( OM = ON \).
