Commercial Mathematics Formulas

1. Simple Interest (SI)

Simple Interest (SI) is calculated using the formula:

\( \text{SI} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} \)

Where:

  • \( P \) = Principal amount
  • \( R \) = Rate of interest per annum
  • \( T \) = Time period in years

2. Compound Interest (CI)

Compound Interest (CI) is calculated using the formula:

\( A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T \)

Compound Interest can be found as:

\( \text{CI} = A - P \)

Where:

  • \( A \) = Amount after interest
  • \( P \) = Principal amount
  • \( R \) = Rate of interest per annum
  • \( T \) = Time period in years

3. Amount (A) in Compound Interest

For different compounding periods, the amount (A) is calculated as:

  • Annually: \( A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T \)
  • Semi-Annually: \( A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{2 \times 100}\right)^{2T} \)
  • Quarterly: \( A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{4 \times 100}\right)^{4T} \)
  • Monthly: \( A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{12 \times 100}\right)^{12T} \)

4. Profit and Loss

Profit or loss is calculated using the formulas:

  • Profit: \( \text{Profit} = \text{Selling Price} - \text{Cost Price} \)
  • Loss: \( \text{Loss} = \text{Cost Price} - \text{Selling Price} \)
  • Profit Percentage: \( \text{Profit %} = \left(\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Cost Price}}\right) \times 100 \)
  • Loss Percentage: \( \text{Loss %} = \left(\frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{Cost Price}}\right) \times 100 \)

5. Discount

Discount and its percentage are calculated using:

  • Discount: \( \text{Discount} = \text{Marked Price} - \text{Selling Price} \)
  • Discount Percentage: \( \text{Discount %} = \left(\frac{\text{Discount}}{\text{Marked Price}}\right) \times 100 \)

6. Marked Price (MP)

The price marked on an article is called the Marked Price (MP), and it can be related to the selling price (SP) by:

\( \text{SP} = \text{MP} - \text{Discount} \)

7. Cost Price (CP)

The original price of an article before selling is called the Cost Price (CP). It can be related to selling price (SP) and profit/loss as:

  • \( \text{CP} = \text{SP} - \text{Profit} \) or \( \text{CP} = \text{SP} + \text{Loss} \)

8. Selling Price (SP)

The price at which an article is sold is called the Selling Price (SP). It can be related to cost price (CP) and profit/loss as:

  • \( \text{SP} = \text{CP} + \text{Profit} \) or \( \text{SP} = \text{CP} - \text{Loss} \)
