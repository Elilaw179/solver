Quadratic Formulas

1. Standard Form of a Quadratic Equation

The standard form of a quadratic equation is:

\( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \)

where \( a \), \( b \), and \( c \) are constants, and \( x \) is the variable.

2. Solutions of a Quadratic Equation (Quadratic Formula)

The solutions (roots) of the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) are given by:

\( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)

This formula gives two solutions, corresponding to the \( \pm \) sign.

3. Discriminant

The discriminant of the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) is given by:

\( \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \)

The nature of the roots depends on the discriminant:

  • If \( \Delta > 0 \), the equation has two distinct real roots.
  • If \( \Delta = 0 \), the equation has exactly one real root (a repeated root).
  • If \( \Delta < 0 \), the equation has two complex roots.

4. Vertex Form of a Quadratic Equation

The vertex form of a quadratic equation is:

\( y = a(x - h)^2 + k \)

where \( (h, k) \) is the vertex of the parabola.

5. Axis of Symmetry

The axis of symmetry of the parabola described by \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) is given by:

\( x = \frac{-b}{2a} \)

This is the vertical line that passes through the vertex of the parabola.

6. Sum and Product of Roots

For the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \), the sum and product of the roots are given by:

Sum of the roots: \( x_1 + x_2 = -\frac{b}{a} \)

Product of the roots: \( x_1 \cdot x_2 = \frac{c}{a} \)

7. Factorization

A quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) can sometimes be factored as:

\( a(x - x_1)(x - x_2) = 0 \)

where \( x_1 \) and \( x_2 \) are the roots of the equation.

8. Completing the Square

The method of completing the square is used to solve the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) by rewriting it in vertex form:

\( ax^2 + bx + c = a(x + \frac{b}{2a})^2 - \frac{b^2 - 4ac}{4a} = 0 \)
