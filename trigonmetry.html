Trigonometry Formulas

1. Trigonometric Ratios

For a right-angled triangle with angle \( \theta \), the trigonometric ratios are defined as:

  • Sine: \( \sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \)
  • Cosine: \( \cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \)
  • Tangent: \( \tan \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} \)

2. Reciprocal Identities

  • Cosecant: \( \csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} \)
  • Secant: \( \sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} \)
  • Cotangent: \( \cot \theta = \frac{1}{\tan \theta} \)

3. Pythagorean Identities

  • \( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \)
  • \( 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \)
  • \( 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \)

4. Angle Sum and Difference Identities

  • \( \sin(A \pm B) = \sin A \cos B \pm \cos A \sin B \)
  • \( \cos(A \pm B) = \cos A \cos B \mp \sin A \sin B \)
  • \( \tan(A \pm B) = \frac{\tan A \pm \tan B}{1 \mp \tan A \tan B} \)

5. Double Angle Identities

  • \( \sin 2A = 2 \sin A \cos A \)
  • \( \cos 2A = \cos^2 A - \sin^2 A \)
  • \( \tan 2A = \frac{2 \tan A}{1 - \tan^2 A} \)

6. Half-Angle Identities

  • \( \sin \frac{A}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos A}{2}} \)
  • \( \cos \frac{A}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 + \cos A}{2}} \)
  • \( \tan \frac{A}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos A}{1 + \cos A}} \) or \( \tan \frac{A}{2} = \frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A} \)

7. Product-to-Sum Identities

  • \( \sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2}[\cos(A - B) - \cos(A + B)] \)
  • \( \cos A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\cos(A - B) + \cos(A + B)] \)
  • \( \sin A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\sin(A + B) + \sin(A - B)] \)

8. Sum-to-Product Identities

  • \( \sin A + \sin B = 2 \sin \left(\frac{A + B}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{A - B}{2}\right) \)
  • \( \sin A - \sin B = 2 \cos \left(\frac{A + B}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{A - B}{2}\right) \)
  • \( \cos A + \cos B = 2 \cos \left(\frac{A + B}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{A - B}{2}\right) \)
  • \( \cos A - \cos B = -2 \sin \left(\frac{A + B}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{A - B}{2}\right) \)

9. Law of Sines

For any triangle with angles \( A \), \( B \), \( C \) and opposite sides \( a \), \( b \), \( c \):

\( \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} \)

10. Law of Cosines

For any triangle with angles \( A \), \( B \), \( C \) and opposite sides \( a \), \( b \), \( c \):

\( c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C \)

\( b^2 = a^2 + c^2 - 2ac \cos B \)

\( a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc \cos A \)

11. Law of Tangents

For any triangle with angles \( A \), \( B \), \( C \) and opposite sides \( a \), \( b \), \( c \):

\( \frac{a - b}{a + b} = \frac{\tan \left(\frac{A - B}{2}\right)}{\tan \left(\frac{A + B}{2}\right)} \)
