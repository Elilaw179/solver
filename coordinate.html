Coordinate Geometry Formulas

1. Distance Formula

The distance between two points \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \):

\[ d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \]

2. Midpoint Formula

The midpoint of the line segment joining two points \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \):

\[ M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right) \]

3. Slope Formula

The slope of the line passing through two points \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \):

\[ m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

4. Equation of a Line (Point-Slope Form)

The equation of a line with slope \( m \) passing through the point \( (x_1, y_1) \):

\[ y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \]

5. Equation of a Line (Slope-Intercept Form)

The equation of a line with slope \( m \) and y-intercept \( c \):

\[ y = mx + c \]

6. Equation of a Line (Two-Point Form)

The equation of a line passing through two points \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \):

\[ y - y_1 = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} (x - x_1) \]

7. Equation of a Line (General Form)

The general equation of a line is:

\[ Ax + By + C = 0 \]

8. Collinearity of Three Points

Three points \( A(x_1, y_1) \), \( B(x_2, y_2) \), and \( C(x_3, y_3) \) are collinear if:

\[ \text{Area of triangle} = \frac{1}{2} \times \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right| = 0 \]

9. Section Formula

The coordinates of a point dividing the line segment joining \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \) in the ratio \( m:n \) internally:

\[ P(x, y) = \left( \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}, \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n} \right) \]

10. Area of a Triangle

The area of a triangle formed by three points \( A(x_1, y_1) \), \( B(x_2, y_2) \), and \( C(x_3, y_3) \):

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right| \]
