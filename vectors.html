Vector Formulas

1. Vector Representation

A vector \( \mathbf{v} \) in 2D space can be represented as:

\( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \) or \( \mathbf{v} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} \)

In 3D space, a vector \( \mathbf{v} \) can be represented as:

\( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix} \) or \( \mathbf{v} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k} \)

2. Magnitude of a Vector

The magnitude (length) of a vector \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \) in 2D space is given by:

\( |\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \)

In 3D space, the magnitude of \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix} \) is:

\( |\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \)

3. Unit Vector

A unit vector in the direction of \( \mathbf{v} \) is given by:

\( \hat{\mathbf{v}} = \frac{\mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|} \)

4. Addition of Vectors

The sum of two vectors \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ y_1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \end{pmatrix} \) in 2D space is:

\( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 + x_2 \\ y_1 + y_2 \end{pmatrix} \)

In 3D space:

\( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 + x_2 \\ y_1 + y_2 \\ z_1 + z_2 \end{pmatrix} \)

5. Subtraction of Vectors

The difference between two vectors \( \mathbf{u} \) and \( \mathbf{v} \) is:

\( \mathbf{u} - \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 - x_2 \\ y_1 - y_2 \end{pmatrix} \) (2D)

\( \mathbf{u} - \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 - x_2 \\ y_1 - y_2 \\ z_1 - z_2 \end{pmatrix} \) (3D)

6. Scalar Multiplication

Multiplying a vector \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \) by a scalar \( c \) gives:

\( c\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} cx \\ cy \end{pmatrix} \) (2D)

In 3D space:

\( c\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} cx \\ cy \\ cz \end{pmatrix} \)

7. Dot Product

The dot product (scalar product) of two vectors \( \mathbf{u} \) and \( \mathbf{v} \) is:

\( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = x_1x_2 + y_1y_2 \) (2D)

In 3D space:

\( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = x_1x_2 + y_1y_2 + z_1z_2 \)

Also, \( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = |\mathbf{u}| |\mathbf{v}| \cos \theta \) where \( \theta \) is the angle between the vectors.

8. Cross Product

The cross product (vector product) of two vectors \( \mathbf{u} \) and \( \mathbf{v} \) in 3D space is:

\( \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} y_1z_2 - z_1y_2 \\ z_1x_2 - x_1z_2 \\ x_1y_2 - y_1x_2 \end{pmatrix} \)

The magnitude of the cross product is given by:

\( |\mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v}| = |\mathbf{u}| |\mathbf{v}| \sin \theta \) where \( \theta \) is the angle between the vectors.

9. Projection of a Vector

The projection of vector \( \mathbf{u} \) on \( \mathbf{v} \) is given by:

\( \text{Proj}_{\mathbf{v}} \mathbf{u} = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|^2} \mathbf{v} \)
