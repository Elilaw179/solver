Logic Formulas

1. Negation

¬P

If P is a proposition, then ¬P (read as "not P") is the negation of P. It is true when P is false, and false when P is true.

2. Conjunction

P ∧ Q

The conjunction of two propositions P and Q is true if and only if both P and Q are true.

3. Disjunction

P ∨ Q

The disjunction of two propositions P and Q is true if at least one of P or Q is true.

4. Implication

P → Q

The implication P → Q (read as "if P then Q") is false only when P is true and Q is false; otherwise, it is true.

5. Biconditional

P ↔ Q

The biconditional P ↔ Q (read as "P if and only if Q") is true if P and Q are either both true or both false.

6. De Morgan's Laws

¬(P ∧ Q) = ¬P ∨ ¬Q

¬(P ∨ Q) = ¬P ∧ ¬Q

De Morgan's laws relate the conjunction and disjunction of two propositions through negation.

7. Tautology

A tautology is a formula that is always true, regardless of the truth values of its components.

P ∨ ¬P = True

8. Contradiction

A contradiction is a formula that is always false, regardless of the truth values of its components.

P ∧ ¬P = False

9. Exclusive OR (XOR)

P ⊕ Q

The exclusive OR of P and Q is true if either P or Q is true, but not both.
