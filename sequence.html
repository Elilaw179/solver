Sequence Formulas

1. Arithmetic Sequence

An arithmetic sequence is a sequence in which each term after the first is obtained by adding a constant difference, \(d\), to the previous term.

  • General Form: \(a_n = a_1 + (n - 1) \cdot d\)
  • Sum of the First \(n\) Terms: \(S_n = \frac{n}{2} \cdot [2a_1 + (n - 1) \cdot d]\)
  • Alternative Sum Formula: \(S_n = \frac{n}{2} \cdot (a_1 + a_n)\)

2. Geometric Sequence

A geometric sequence is a sequence in which each term after the first is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a constant ratio, \(r\).

  • General Form: \(a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}\)
  • Sum of the First \(n\) Terms: \(S_n = a_1 \cdot \frac{1 - r^n}{1 - r}\) (for \(r \neq 1\))
  • Sum to Infinity (for \(|r| < 1\)): \(S_\infty = \frac{a_1}{1 - r}\)

3. Harmonic Sequence

A harmonic sequence is a sequence of numbers where the reciprocal of the terms forms an arithmetic sequence.

  • General Form: \(a_n = \frac{1}{a_1 + (n - 1) \cdot d}\)

4. Fibonacci Sequence

A Fibonacci sequence is a sequence where each term is the sum of the two preceding ones, usually starting with 0 and 1.

  • General Form: \(F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}\), where \(F_1 = 0\) and \(F_2 = 1\)

5. Quadratic Sequence

A quadratic sequence is a sequence where the second differences between consecutive terms are constant.

  • General Form: \(a_n = an^2 + bn + c\)
