1. Product Law

The logarithm of a product is equal to the sum of the logarithms of the factors.

Formula: \( \log_b(m \times n) = \log_b m + \log_b n \)

Example: \( \log_2(4 \times 8) = \log_2 4 + \log_2 8 = 2 + 3 = 5 \)

2. Quotient Law

The logarithm of a quotient is equal to the difference of the logarithms.

Formula: \( \log_b\left(\frac{m}{n}\right) = \log_b m - \log_b n \)

Example: \( \log_3\left(\frac{9}{3}\right) = \log_3 9 - \log_3 3 = 2 - 1 = 1 \)

3. Power Law

The logarithm of a number raised to a power is equal to the power times the logarithm of the number.

Formula: \( \log_b(m^n) = n \times \log_b m \)

Example: \( \log_2(8^2) = 2 \times \log_2 8 = 2 \times 3 = 6 \)

4. Change of Base Formula

You can change the base of a logarithm by using the logarithms in other bases.

Formula: \( \log_b m = \frac{\log_k m}{\log_k b} \)

Example: \( \log_2 8 = \frac{\log_{10} 8}{\log_{10} 2} = \frac{0.903}{0.301} = 3 \)

5. Logarithm of 1

The logarithm of 1 to any base is always 0.

Formula: \( \log_b 1 = 0 \)

Example: \( \log_5 1 = 0 \)

6. Logarithm of the Base

The logarithm of the base itself is always 1.

Formula: \( \log_b b = 1 \)

Example: \( \log_7 7 = 1 \)
