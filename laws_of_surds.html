1. Multiplication Law

When two surds are multiplied, the result is the square root of the product of the numbers.

Formula: \( \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a \times b} \)

Example: \( \sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{3} = \sqrt{6} \)

2. Division Law

When one surd is divided by another, the result is the square root of the quotient of the numbers.

Formula: \( \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} \)

Example: \( \frac{\sqrt{8}}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{4} = 2 \)

3. Addition and Subtraction Law

Like surds (those with the same radicand) can be added or subtracted by combining their coefficients.

Formula: \( a\sqrt{b} + c\sqrt{b} = (a + c)\sqrt{b} \)

Example: \( 2\sqrt{5} + 3\sqrt{5} = 5\sqrt{5} \)

4. Simplification Law

A surd can be simplified by expressing the radicand as a product of a square number.

Formula: \( \sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} \)

Example: \( \sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \times 2} = \sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2} \)

5. Rationalizing the Denominator

To remove a surd from the denominator, multiply both the numerator and the denominator by the conjugate of the denominator.

Formula: \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{a}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{a} \)

Example: \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \)

6. Law of Conjugates

The product of a binomial surd and its conjugate is a rational number.

Formula: \( (a + \sqrt{b})(a - \sqrt{b}) = a^2 - b \)

Example: \( (2 + \sqrt{3})(2 - \sqrt{3}) = 4 - 3 = 1 \)
